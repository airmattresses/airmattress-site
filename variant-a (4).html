Air Mattress Buyer's Guide: What to Look For

Not every air mattress is created equal. Here's what to prioritize based on how you plan to use it.

üìè

Height

Aim for 18" or taller. It feels more like a real bed and is significantly easier to get in and out of ‚Äî especially for older guests or anyone with mobility concerns. Our top pick offers 13", 16", and 20" options ‚Äî the only brand on this list with three height tiers.

‚ö°

Pump Speed

Built-in electric pumps are standard now, but inflation speed varies dramatically. The fastest we tested (King Koil) inflates a full queen in under 2 minutes. The slowest takes over 5. If you're setting up multiple beds, that difference adds up fast.

üß±

Construction Type

Two types: beam construction (basic channels) and coil-beam (individual air-filled coils). Coil-beam distributes weight more evenly, reduces the "rolling to the center" effect, and provides noticeably better spinal support.

üí®

Air Retention

All air mattresses lose some air overnight ‚Äî that's physics. The difference is how much. Premium models with tighter seam construction and better valve design lose significantly less. We measured pressure loss across 48 hours and the variance was larger than expected.

Weight Capacity

If two adults will share the mattress, aim for 600 lbs minimum. Most quality models hit this mark ‚Äî don't overlook it when comparing options.

Warranty & Returns

A 1-year warranty is the category standard. King Koil goes further ‚Äî free shipping, free returns, and a 1-year warranty. The best coverage we found at this price point.
